Tennet Project for Geries Engineers

I was associated in a project which was about underground cable route construction by digitalization the data, georeferencing, GIS-Analysis (such as data query, calculating the fields, where located in/outside of our edge-strip with 2 m of buffer, transforming the fields to polygons, and calculated the geometry of cable-fields by ArcGIS) and also land use mapping. In this project we used mainly ArcGIS 9/10 and tried to convert the project to QGIS e.g. Using QGIS to produce a symmetric difference of the rest-fields.

Assessing erosion in Madagascar

In this project, we assessed the risk of a special erosion surfaces in Madagascar called Lavakas, which occurs after deforestation, by using different data such as Aster DEM with resolution of 30x30, Satellite images of Sentinel 2, Soil characteristic data as well as precipitation data to analyse the area of the project. Based on these data, we used QGIS and ArcGIS to create maps of height, inclination, orientation, SAVI (Soil Adjusted Vegetation Index), soil thickness, precipitation, and wind speed. We then analysed the generated maps using R Studio and SAGA, developed various diagrams to determine the parameters most influential on erosion. This analysis culminated in the creation of Risk and Hazard Maps.

Nekachoob

I investigated the physical and financial flow of production units within the Nekachoob Industrial Complex to identify and eliminate losses, optimize raw wood usage, and reduce production costs including labour, administrative financial and support costs, increase production efficiency and optimize the use of all usable waste. Analyzing waste debris (WD), which ranged from 6% to 70% across units and comprised 44% of the complex’s total wood input, revealed that utilizing this waste could reduce overall costs by up to 22%. This study demonstrated the economic and environmental benefits of WD recycling within wood industries.
